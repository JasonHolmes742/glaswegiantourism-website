  • Botanic Gardens:Founded in 1817 by Thomas Hopkirk a glaswegian botanist founded glasgow botanic gardens with the support of a number of local dignitaries and the university of glasgow.​
  • Kelvingrove Art Gallery & Museum:The Kelvingrove Art Gallery and Museum opened on May 2,1901.​State of the art galleries displaying an astonishing 8000 objects.
  • Riverside Museum:Founded on April 14 1964 as Glasgow Museum of Transport. The museum has had 3 locations in its history. It was intially located at the former Coplawhill tram depot on Albert Drive in Pollokshields before moving to Kelvinhall in 1988. The Kelvinhall site itself closed in april 2010, with the museum moving to its third home at the riverside museum in June 2011.​
  • University of Glasgow:Rich history. Founded in 1451 The University of Glasgow is the second oldest university in Scotland after St Andrews and is the fourth oldest university in the english speaking world.​
  • Ashton Lane:Ashton lane is a cobbled backstreet in the west end of glasgow. It is connected to Byres Road by a short linking lane beside Hillhead subway station.It is noted for its bars, restaurants and even has a licensed cinema.​​
  • Pollok Country Park:Country park that has plenty of highland cattle to see and is also home to The Burrell Collection Museum.
  • Glasgow Central Mosque:Glasgow Central Mosque is located on the south bank of the river clyde in the gorbals district of central glasgow.The islamic centre is the central institution for the local muslim community, and is the largest community centre in strathclyde. The mosque itself is the largest mosque in Scotland.​​
  • St Andrew's Cathedral:The metropolitan cathedral church of saint andrew is a latin catholic cathedral in the city centre of glasgow scotland.It is the mother church of the archdiocese of glasgow founded by Saint Mungo.​​The cathedral is dedicated to Saint Andrew patron saint of Scotland.​
  • Glasgow Murals Trail:Glasgow has 29 murals in and around the city centre. There is a glasgow mural trail with an online website dedicated to the murals. ​
